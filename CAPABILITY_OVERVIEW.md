# Platform Capability Overview

*Adapted from the platform's client-facing capability presentation.*

## One platform to manage your entire business

Built to unify Sales, Inventory, Accounts, Procurement, and Manufacturing under one system — with real-time visibility across every branch and depot, multi-company and multi-department support, and access from PC, tablet, or mobile. In production for manufacturing groups for 2+ years.

## Core modules

**Purchase & Procurement**
Streamlined supplier management with automated purchase workflows. Supports partial and full GRN (Goods Receipt Note) processing, with pending-item and return tracking to keep stock records accurate.

**Sales & Distribution**
Fast order-to-invoice processing with full Challan, Invoice, and Gate Pass tracking. Undelivered-goods reporting helps catch and reduce losses before they compound; supports quick and supplementary invoice generation.

**Inventory Management**
Real-time stock visibility across every depot and plant. Inventory-ageing reporting flags dead or slow-moving stock; item transfer, issue, and return tracking stay fully auditable. Movement-frequency data supports smarter sourcing decisions.

**General Accounting**
An integrated chart of accounts tied directly to sales, purchase, and inventory transactions — trial balance, P&L, and balance sheet generate automatically, year-end processing is simplified, and duplicate manual entry is eliminated by design.

## Reliability & security

- Built on a Java + MS SQL Server foundation for scalability and reliability
- Fully device-responsive — accessible from anywhere
- Automated backups with high availability
- Multi-business and multi-organization access control
- Role-based permissions with full audit logging

## Reporting & insight

- Daily, monthly, and yearly sales reporting
- Invoice-wise gross profit reporting
- Pending and undelivered-order reporting
- Inventory ageing and movement-frequency analysis
- Full MIS accounting report suite

## Rollout approach

Designed for low-disruption adoption: start with a single company or department, then scale gradually. Includes data migration and legacy-system integration support, hands-on staff training, and a local support team for ongoing troubleshooting and updates.
